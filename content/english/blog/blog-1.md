If you run a restaurant today, you've probably noticed something: more and more of your customers want to order from their phones. Not just for delivery — for everything. Picking up a lunch order on their way to work. Scanning a QR code at the table to skip the wait. Placing a dinner order ahead of time so it's ready the moment they walk in.

This isn't a passing fad. Online food ordering has become the default way people interact with restaurants, and the numbers back it up. In 2025, over 70% of consumers in Europe placed at least one online food order per month. That figure keeps climbing. If your restaurant doesn't offer a simple, fast digital ordering experience, you're leaving money on the table — literally.

### Why Customers Prefer Online Ordering

Let's be honest: nobody loves waiting in line or being put on hold to place a phone order. Online ordering removes that friction entirely. Your customers browse the menu at their own pace, customize their meals, and pay in seconds. No misheard toppings, no awkward hold music.

But it goes deeper than convenience. Today's diners expect a seamless experience across every channel — whether they're ordering for **delivery**, using **click and collect**, or scanning a **QR code to order at the table**. Restaurants that meet those expectations earn repeat business. Those that don't get passed over for the place down the street that does.

### The Real Benefits for Restaurant Owners

Adopting an online ordering system isn't just about keeping up with trends. It directly impacts your bottom line:

- **Fewer order mistakes** — When customers select their own dishes and options on screen, misunderstandings disappear. That means less food waste and happier guests.
- **Higher average order value** — Digital menus make it easy to suggest add-ons, upsells, and combos. Most restaurants see a 15–25% increase in ticket size with online orders.
- **Better staff efficiency** — Your team spends less time taking orders over the phone and more time delivering great service where it matters.
- **Smarter operations** — Pre-orders and scheduled pickups let you anticipate busy periods and prep accordingly, reducing kitchen stress during peak hours.

### The Commission Trap: Why Platform Choice Matters

Here's where a lot of restaurant owners get burned. The big delivery marketplaces make it easy to get started, but their commission fees — often 25% to 30% per order — eat into your margins fast. On a €20 order, you might be handing over €6 just for the privilege of being listed.

That math doesn't work for most independent restaurants. And worse, those platforms own the customer relationship. The data, the reviews, the reorder prompts — they all belong to the marketplace, not to you.

That's why more and more restaurateurs are switching to **commission-free online ordering platforms** like Piki. You get your own branded ordering system — for delivery, click & collect, and dine-in — without giving up a cut of every sale. You keep your margins, your customer data, and full control over your brand.

### QR Code Table Ordering: The Quiet Revolution

One trend that's exploded since 2024 is **QR code ordering for dine-in**. Customers scan a code at their table, browse the menu on their phone, and place their order without waiting for a server. It's faster for them and frees up your staff to focus on hospitality rather than order-taking.

The best part? It works for every type of restaurant — from fast-casual spots to sit-down dining. And because orders go straight to the kitchen, there's no room for miscommunication.

### Getting Started Is Easier Than You Think

If you've been putting off adding online ordering to your restaurant, now is the time. The technology is mature, setup is fast, and the payoff is immediate. Whether you start with click & collect, add QR code ordering for dine-in, or go all-in with delivery — every digital order is a chance to grow your revenue without growing your overhead.

The restaurants that thrive in 2026 and beyond won't be the ones with the biggest kitchens or the most tables. They'll be the ones that make it ridiculously easy for customers to order, pay, and come back for more.
